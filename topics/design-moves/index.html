How This Catalog Works

Staff+ design is a small vocabulary of recurring moves applied to varied problems. The skill is recognizing the trigger (the bottleneck shape that calls for this move), not memorizing the mechanism (which is a lookup). Each page in this catalog leads with the trigger, makes the mechanism a one-paragraph reference, and links to scenarios that exercise the move.

Pages are extracted just-in-time: a move gets its own page after the second scenario reuses it. That way each page reflects the actual triggers we've encountered, not theoretical ones.

Coming Just-in-Time

The following moves are extracted to their own page on the second reuse across scenarios — not before. Until extracted, scenarios cite the appropriate bottleneck entry's "first move" link, which gives the trigger and the mechanism inline.

  • Subsetting — deterministic backend subsets per caller; the canonical defense against connection saturation.
  • Power-of-Two-Choices (P2C) — load balancing by sampling 2 backends and picking the less-loaded; pairs with EWMA latency weighting.
  • Consistent hashing variants — ring vs rendezvous vs Maglev; comparison page (not three pages).
  • Sharding strategies — hash vs range vs lookup-table; when each beats the other.
  • Watermarks & late arrival — streaming windowing under out-of-order events.
  • Negative caching & refresh-ahead — invalid-key floods + predictably-hot keys; pairs with single-flight.
  • Sketch mergeability — why associativity matters; the property that lets sketches scale.
  • Backpressure & shedding — producer faster than consumer; queue depth as autoscale signal; degradation contract.
  • Tail tolerance — hedged requests, speculative execution, P2C; the catalogue of latency-tail mitigations.
  • Anti-patterns museum — named anti-patterns with the bottleneck they fail to address.
