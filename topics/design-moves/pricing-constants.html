Pricing Constants & Hardware Capacities (M12)

The canonical cost-and-capacity reference every C7 cost chain and BOE math derivation cites. Without this, dollar numbers and capacity numbers drift across scenario pages and aren't comparable.

~8 min reference

How to Use These Numbers

  • Order-of-magnitude reference. These are AWS US-East on-demand list prices as of 2026 — use them to ground BOE math, not as quotes. Real procurement is ±50% off list (reserved, savings plans, EDP, GCP/Azure cross-shop).
  • One source for every scenario. Every scenario page's $/mo derivation cites this page. If a scenario needs a number not here, add it here first, not inline on the scenario.
  • Cite the line, not the bottom-line. "8 H100 GPUs × $12.29/hr × 720 hr/mo = $70,790/mo" is auditable. "$70k/mo for compute" is not.
  • Update cadence. Re-anchor to current list prices once a year. Per-row last-checked date below where it matters.

Pricing modifiers

  • Reserved (1-yr no-upfront): ~30% off list.
  • Reserved (3-yr all-upfront): ~55% off list.
  • Savings Plans (Compute, 1-yr): ~28% off; (3-yr): ~52% off; flexible across instance families.
  • Spot: 60–90% off, with interruption risk. Right for fault-tolerant batch (training, transcoding, indexing); wrong for stateful or latency-critical.
  • Enterprise Discount Program (EDP): large customers negotiate 10–25% off everything; not modeled here.

Cost-per-month conversion

One instance running 24×7 = 720 hours/month. $/hr × 720 = $/month for one always-on instance.

  • r6i.4xlarge: $1.01 × 720 = $727/month per instance.
  • p5.48xlarge: $98.32 × 720 = $70,790/month per node (8 H100).
  • One H100 / mo at on-demand: $12.29 × 720 = $8,849/month.

The egress trap

Egress to Internet is ~9× more expensive than cross-region replication and ~9× more expensive than inter-AZ. Designs that fan out user-facing data inside one AZ are vastly cheaper than designs that egress every read.

  • Always classify each byte: does this byte cross an AZ boundary? Cross a region? Hit the Internet? Each crossing has a different price.
  • For user-facing reads at scale, CDN is mandatory (CloudFront cuts the per-GB cost by ~30% for hot content; cache hits are $0).

Cheat Sheet: Order-of-Magnitude $/mo

For BOE math, memorize these ballpark monthly costs so you can sanity-check derivations on the fly:

  • 1 always-on r6i.4xlarge ≈ $700/mo (memory-heavy general-purpose).
  • 1 always-on H100 ≈ $9,000/mo on-demand; ~$4,000/mo on 3-yr reserved.
  • 1 always-on A100 (40 GB) ≈ $3,000/mo.
  • 1 PB S3 Standard ≈ $24,000/mo.
  • 1 PB S3 Glacier Deep Archive ≈ $1,000/mo.
  • 1 TB Internet egress ≈ $90 (first 10 TB) → $50 (above 150 TB).
  • 1 TB cross-region transfer ≈ $20.
  • 1 TB inter-AZ (round-trip) ≈ $20 ($10 each way).
  • 1 always-on Redis (104 GB cache.r6g.4xlarge) ≈ $1,000/mo per node.
  • 1 always-on Kafka broker (m5.4xlarge) ≈ $600/mo + storage.
  • 1 B DynamoDB writes ≈ $1,250.
  • 1 B DynamoDB reads ≈ $250.
  • 1 B Lambda invocations ≈ $200 + GB-sec compute.

Use these as anchors. If your final design's $/mo is far off from what these constants would suggest, something is wrong in the chain.

Anti-Patterns in Cost Math

  • Mixing list price and reserved price. Pick one basis (list, or reserved-with-discount-X) and apply it consistently across the whole design. Inconsistent basis hides 30–50% cost differences.
  • Forgetting cross-AZ. Many designs assume "same region = free network" — wrong. Inter-AZ is $0.01/GB each way, easily $thousands/mo at scale.
  • Per-request pricing assumed too low. S3 PUTs at $5/M sound cheap; at 10k events/sec sustained ≈ 26 B events/mo = $130k/mo just in PUTs.
  • Ignoring storage I/O for managed services. Aurora and DynamoDB charge per I/O on top of per-GB-month. A read-heavy workload's I/O bill can exceed the storage bill.
  • Counting CDN as free. CloudFront has a per-GB price (lower than direct egress, but not zero). Cache hits are free; misses cost.
  • Aggregating across regions without naming the path. "Total egress = X TB/mo" is meaningless; the price depends on where it goes (Internet vs cross-region vs inter-AZ).
