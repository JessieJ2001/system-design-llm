K8 — Pipeline Parallelism (split layers across domains)

How do you build a scalable across-domain split — when the model exceeds a single NVLink domain even at TP=N_dom — without the pipeline bubble eating the throughput you split for? This page derives the bubble formula for serving (where micro-batches are in-flight requests, not a fixed training batch), and bounds it with the schedule choice and the per-stage memory ceiling.

SLIs This Page Reduces

SLIs this page reduces (the reverse index of K1 §3.12):
  • Feasibility (model fits) — splitting layers across NVLink domains makes a model resident that no single domain can hold even at TP=N_dom.
  • Throughput — PP adds pipeline stages that run concurrently — net of the bubble the schedule choice (D24) minimises.
  • TPOT — bubble-taxed: PP raises single-stream latency; the page bounds, not removes, that tax.
  • Feasibility (per-stage HBM) — D38 keeps each stage from OOMing on in-flight-microbatch KV+activations before compute even binds.

The PP Spine (stated up front, before §1)

This page is a child of K6 and resolves the doesn’t-fit-past-one-domain binding of K6’s grid. The chain:

  • Model exceeds one NVLink domain even at TP=N_dom → split layers across domains/nodes = PP.
  • New problem: stages idle waiting for the pipe to fill/drain — the bubble.
  • Micro-batching shrinks it — but in serving the micro-batches are in-flight requests (variable, often few) ⇒ the serving bubble is structurally larger than the training formula implies.
  • Uneven layer cost (MoE / embedding+LM-head) imbalances stages ⇒ the slowest stage gates the pipe.
  • And: each stage holds KV+act for all in-flight micro-batches ⇒ a deep pipeline OOMs before compute binds (D38).
  • ⇒ pick the schedule + depth that minimise the bubble at serving batch sizes (D24), cut on block boundaries (K0/K18).

1. The Problem It Solves

Problem→Solution stub. Problem: the model’s weights exceed what one NVLink domain holds even at TP=N_dom — TP can’t go wider without the all-reduce crossing IB (cite K7/D26). Solution (what the page builds): split the layers across domains/nodes so each domain holds a contiguous block range, P2P-handing the residual stream forward — trading a fill/drain bubble for feasibility, then bounding that bubble.number: model bytes vs per-domain budget at TP=N_dom on the trace → required PP stages — cites K1 §3.2 (#memory), K20 (N_dom) · canonical: gpu-inference-cost-shapes.html

The serving bubble, derived (the page’s home — not the training formula)

Problem→Solution stub (the page’s opening derivation, sub-bulleted). Problem: the textbook bubble fraction assumes a large fixed training batch. Solution, derived for serving: (a) bubble fraction = (P−1)/(P−1+m) for P stages, m micro-batches (formula is K1’s — cite it); (b) training m is large (a fixed global batch) → small bubble; (c) serving m = the in-flight requests the scheduler can present as micro-batches — small and variable ⇒ the serving bubble is structurally larger than the formula implies, shrinking only as continuous-batching concurrency (K4) rises; (d) the stage cut must fall on a block boundary (K0/K18) — no mid-residual split.number: bubble fraction at training m vs serving m on the trace (P=4, m=2–8) — cites K1 (the bubble formula), K4 (concurrency supplies m), K0/K18 (legal cut)

Deep dive D24 — serving-PP bubble at low/variable batch + uneven-layer stage imbalance

Deep-dive stub. Problem: (a) low/variable serving m → big bubble; (b) MoE / embedding+LM-head layers cost far more than a plain block → equal layer-count stages have unequal time → the slowest stage gates the pipe. Solution: interleaved-1F1B (each GPU owns non-contiguous layer chunks → finer micro-batch granularity → smaller bubble) + cost-balanced (not count-balanced) stage partitioning. Crossover: interleave depth trades bubble reduction for extra P2P comm.number: bubble at serving m with/without interleaving on the trace; the time skew of an MoE stage vs a plain stage — cites K1 (bubble), K9 (MoE layer cost) · real: Megatron interleaved-1F1B, DeepSpeed-Inference + URLs

Deep dive D38 — PP stage-memory & the in-flight-activation footprint

Deep-dive stub (a real OOM-before-compute trap). Problem: every stage must simultaneously hold KV + activations for all in-flight micro-batches (pipeline depth × per-request state) → a deep pipeline multiplies resident memory and OOMs a stage before compute is the limit. Solution: PP depth is bounded by per-stage memory, not only the bubble; cap in-flight micro-batches or shrink per-request KV (K2). Crossover (formula): P_max = HBM_stage / (weights/P + maxflight·(KV+act)/P) — both directions.number: P_max on the trace at L1/L3 in-flight counts — cites K1 #memory, K2 (KV shrink), K18 (activation footprint) · real: DeepSpeed-Inference, vLLM PP + URLs

Rule 11 — the schedule spectrum

Selection-sweep stub. 4. Alternatives & When Each Wins

Stub (≥2, disqualifying constraint per alt). (a) More TP instead of PP — disq: TP caps at N_dom; the all-reduce can’t cross IB (cite K7/D26); wins only within one domain.
